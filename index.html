Roberto Gómez Bolaños

CH

Born in Mexico City on February 21, 1929 and died in Cancún, Quintana Roo on November 28, 2014, Roberto Gómez Bolaños was a Mexican actor, comedian, playwright, writer, screenwriter, composer, director and television producer. He is widely considered one of the icons of Spanish-speaking humor and entertainment and one of the greatest comedians of all time.

He is recognized throughout the planet for writing, directing and starring in the television series Chespirito (1970-1973, 1980-1995), El Chavo del Ocho (1973-1980) and El Chapulín Colorado (1973-1979). The character of El Chavo is one of the most iconic in Latin American television history, and El Chavo del Ocho continues to be immensely popular, with a daily global audience averaging 91 million viewers.

According to the newspaper El País, he is considered a comedian who created characters that amused not only several generations of Mexicans but millions of people in fifty countries. He gained international notoriety for having been the creator and protagonist of the television series El Chavo del 8 and El Chapulín Colorado and with the Chespirito Program that won the title of number 1 program in comedy television, which earned him great prestige and it earned him recognition as one of the most respected comic writers of all time. The versatile man studied engineering, but his true talent was found in the artistic world, which is why he never formally practiced the engineering profession.
